Cyclic Group

Cyclic Group

A cyclic group is a group \(G\) that can be generated by a single element. That is, there exists an element \(g \in G\) such that every element of \(G\) can be expressed as a power of \(g\).

Formal Definition

A group \((G, \cdot)\) is cyclic if there exists \(g \in G\) such that \[G = \{g^n : n \in \mathbb{Z}\}\]

We write \(G = \langle g \rangle\) and say that \(g\) generates \(G\) or that \(g\) is a generator of \(G\).

Notation for Powers

In a cyclic group \(G = \langle g \rangle\): - \(g^0 = e\) (the identity element) - \(g^n = \underbrace{g \cdot g \cdot \ldots \cdot g}_{n \text{ times}}\) for positive \(n\) - \(g^{-n} = (g^{-1})^n\) for positive \(n\)

For additive groups, we use: - \(ng\) instead of \(g^n\) - \(0g = 0\) (the additive identity) - \((-n)g = n(-g)\) for positive \(n\)

Order of a Cyclic Group

A cyclic group can be: - Finite: If the cardinality \(|G| = n\) for some positive integer \(n\), then \(G = \{e, g, g^2, \ldots, g^{n-1}\}\) - Infinite: If no positive power of \(g\) equals the identity

Important Properties

  1. Abelian: Every cyclic group is abelian (commutative)

    • For any \(a, b \in G\), we have \(a = g^m\) and \(b = g^n\) for some \(m, n \in \mathbb{Z}\)
    • Thus \(ab = g^m g^n = g^{m+n} = g^{n+m} = g^n g^m = ba\)

  2. Subgroups: Every subgroup of a cyclic group is cyclic

  3. Isomorphism Classes:

    • Every infinite cyclic group is isomorphic to \((\mathbb{Z}, +)\)
    • Every finite cyclic group of order \(n\) is isomorphic to \((\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}, +)\)

  4. Generators:

    • A cyclic group of order \(n\) has \(\phi(n)\) generators, where \(\phi\) is Euler‚Äôs totient function (which can be computed using the Inclusion-Exclusion Principle)
    • For infinite cyclic groups, only \(g\) and \(g^{-1}\) are generators

Examples

Finite Cyclic Groups

  • \(\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}\) under addition, generated by \(\overline{1}\)
  • The group of \(n\)-th roots of unity under multiplication, generated by \(e^{2\pi i/n}\)
  • The rotation group of a regular \(n\)-gon

Infinite Cyclic Groups

  • \((\mathbb{Z}, +)\) generated by \(1\) (or by \(-1\))
  • The group of powers of 2 under multiplication: \(\{2^n : n \in \mathbb{Z}\}\)

Classification

The classification of cyclic groups is particularly simple: - Up to isomorphism, there is exactly one cyclic group of each order (finite or infinite) - Two cyclic groups are isomorphic if and only if they have the same order

Products of Cyclic Groups

The direct product of cyclic groups has special properties: - \(\mathbb{Z}_m \times \mathbb{Z}_n\) is cyclic if and only if \(\gcd(m,n) = 1\) - When \(\gcd(m,n) = 1\), we have \(\mathbb{Z}_m \times \mathbb{Z}_n \cong \mathbb{Z}_{mn}\) - In general, \(\mathbb{Z}_m \times \mathbb{Z}_n \cong \mathbb{Z}_{\gcd(m,n)} \times \mathbb{Z}_{\text{lcm}(m,n)}\) (see @../number-theory/def-lcm)
