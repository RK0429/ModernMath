Simple Group

Simple Group

A simple group is a non-trivial group whose only normal subgroups are the trivial subgroup \(\{e\}\) and the group itself \(G\).

Formal Definition

A group \(G\) is simple if: 1. \(G \neq \{e\}\) (the group is non-trivial) 2. The only normal subgroups of \(G\) are \(\{e\}\) and \(G\)

Equivalent Characterizations

A group \(G\) is simple if and only if: - \(G\) has no proper non-trivial normal subgroups - For any normal subgroup \(N \triangleleft G\), either \(N = \{e\}\) or \(N = G\) - The only quotient groups of \(G\) are \(G/\{e\} \cong G\) and \(G/G \cong \{e\}\)

Importance

Simple groups are fundamental in group theory because: - They serve as the ‚Äúbuilding blocks‚Äù of all finite groups - The Jordan-H√∂lder theorem shows that every finite group can be broken down into a composition series of simple groups - They cannot be decomposed into smaller groups via the quotient construction

Classification

Abelian Simple Groups

The only abelian simple groups are the cyclic groups of prime order: - \(\mathbb{Z}_p\) where \(p\) is prime

This follows from Lagrange‚Äôs theorem: a group of prime order has no proper non-trivial subgroups.

Non-Abelian Simple Groups

The complete classification of finite simple groups includes:

  1. Cyclic groups of prime order: \(\mathbb{Z}_p\)
  2. Alternating groups: \(A_n\) for \(n \geq 5\)
  3. Groups of Lie type: Classical groups over finite fields
  4. Sporadic groups: 26 exceptional groups

Examples

Simple Groups

  • \(\mathbb{Z}_2, \mathbb{Z}_3, \mathbb{Z}_5, \ldots\) (cyclic groups of prime order)
  • \(A_5\) (the alternating group on 5 elements, smallest non-abelian simple group with 60 elements)
  • \(A_n\) for all \(n \geq 5\)
  • \(PSL(2, 7)\) (projective special linear group with 168 elements)

Non-Simple Groups

  • \(\mathbb{Z}_4\) has \(\{0, 2\}\) as a proper normal subgroup
  • \(S_n\) for \(n \geq 2\) has \(A_n\) as a proper normal subgroup
  • Any abelian group of composite order

Properties

  1. Simplicity and Homomorphisms: If \(\phi: G \to H\) is a homomorphism from a simple group \(G\), then either:

    • \(\phi\) is trivial (maps everything to identity), or
    • \(\phi\) is injective (by the first isomorphism theorem)

  2. Center: The center of a non-abelian simple group is trivial

  3. Minimal Normal Subgroups: A simple group is a minimal normal subgroup of any group containing it
